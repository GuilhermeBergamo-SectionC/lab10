PARTS OF A RAILGUN

Power supply

The power supply is simply a source of electric current. Typically, the current used in medium- to large-caliber rail guns is in the millions of amps. Just so you know how much energy a railgun needs, a railgun needs at least 25 MW of energy, and that would be enough to power 20000 homes.

Rail

The rail assembly is critical in guiding the projectile and conducting the massive electrical current. This component's design and material are vital for efficiency and durability. If the material is not good enough, it may break with 1 shot from the railgun.

The armature

The armature bridges the gap between the rails. It can be a solid piece of conductive metal or a conductive sabot -- a carrier that houses a dart or other projectile. Some rail guns use a plasma armature. In this set-up a thin metal foil is placed on the back of a non-conducting projectile. When power flows through this foil it vaporizes and becomes a plasma, which carries the current.

How the railgun actually works

A railgun uses electrical energy to generate a magnetic field between two parallel rails. When a current flows through these rails and a conductive projectile, it creates an electromagnetic force. This force accelerates the projectile along the rails at high speeds. The projectile is then launched with enough velocity to reach its target without traditional explosives.
